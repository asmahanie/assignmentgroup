Fascination in Perlis

Perlis, also known by its honorific title Perlis Indera Kayangan, is the smallest state in Malaysia in terms of both area and population. Located on the northwestern coast of Peninsular Malaysia, it shares borders with the Thai provinces of Satun and Songkhla to the north, and the Malaysian state of Kedah to the south. Despite its small size, Perlis has a unique rustic charm. The state is known for its lush green rice fields, peaceful traditional villages and the untouched natural beauty of its forested border with Thailand.

The introduction of the
state of Perlis.

The name of the State of Perlis is said to come from the Siamese word prow-lowy which means 'drift coconut', from the Northern Malay dialect word 'perelus' which means 'the foot is included in or into the crevice of something'.
